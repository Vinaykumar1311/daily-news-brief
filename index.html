REGISTRATION WITHOUT RITUALS, CEREMONIES DOESN'T VALIDATE HINDU MARRIAGE: GUJARAT HIGH COURT

The Gujarat High Court has ruled that the mere registration of a marriage without performing essential religious rituals and ceremonies does not validate a Hindu marriage. In its order, the court emphasized that the performance of customary ceremonies, such as the saptapadi, remains the legal foundation of a Hindu marriage. This ruling was made during a case where a marriage certificate was obtained without any traditional rites being conducted. The court clarified that statutory registration cannot substitute or bypass the essential religious rites required under Hindu law. Consequently, marriages registered solely for administrative convenience without actual ceremonies will not be recognized as legally binding under the Hindu Marriage Act.
